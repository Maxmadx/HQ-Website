# Squarespace SEO Checklist

Source: [Squarespace Help Center — Liste de vérification SEO](https://support.squarespace.com/hc/fr-fr/articles/360002090267-Liste-de-v%C3%A9rification-SEO)

A summary of every point from the Squarespace SEO checklist, translated and condensed.

---

## 1. Before Publishing

### 1.1 Site Information
- **Add a site title** — keep under 60 characters and include relevant keywords.
- **Add a site description** — 50–300 characters, readable and relevant to the site.
- **Add SEO descriptions** — give every page its own unique description (50–300 characters).
- **Check page and title formatting** — control how titles appear in browser tabs, social shares, and search results.
- **Run the SEO Report tool** — scans the site for missing alt text and page descriptions.

### 1.2 Site Design
- **Add a browser icon (favicon)** — appears in browser tabs and search results.
- **Add social sharing images** — shown when pages are shared on social networks.
- **Create a custom 404 page** — link to popular sections so lost visitors stay on the site.
- **Review your content** — follow content best practices on every page (see Section 4).

### 1.3 Domain and URL Configuration
- **Connect a custom domain** — replace the default `.squarespace.com` URL.
- **Connect to social networks** — enables social sharing and encourages content distribution.
- **Verify SSL is enabled** — make sure the "Secure" setting is on; non-SSL sites may be penalised.
- **Check URL slugs** — slugs should describe the page, not be random characters.
- **Choose a blog post URL format** — pick a format that includes the post title in auto-generated URLs.

### 1.4 Location Information
- **Add your address** — improves visibility in region-specific searches.
- **Publish your address online** — create a Google Business Profile listing.

---

## 2. After Publishing

- **Verify the site with Google Search Console** — manage how the site appears in Google search results.
- **Request Google indexing** — ask Google to crawl the site and refresh its index.
- **Verify the site with Bing Webmaster Tools** — covers Bing and Yahoo results.
- **Connect to Google Analytics** — track visits and review traffic reports.

---

## 3. As the Site Evolves

### 3.1 Optimise New Content
- **Make strategic updates** — keep applying content best practices to new pages.
- **Redirect changed or broken links** — set up URL redirects whenever a slug changes.
- **Update and add new content regularly** — signals to search engines that the site is active.

### 3.2 Track Site Traffic
- **Use site analytics tools** — monitor search keywords, activity logs, popular content, and traffic patterns.
- **Follow Google Analytics** — refine visitor tracking and data analysis over time.

---

## 4. Content Best Practices

### 4.1 Text Content
- **Use strategic keywords** — match the words visitors are likely to search for.
- **Structure content with headings** — easier for both humans and search engines to scan.

### 4.2 Images
- **Add alt text to images** — improves accessibility and helps search engines understand content.
- **Use readable image filenames** — filenames act as fallback alt text; follow naming conventions.
- **Keep pages responsive and lightweight** — images under 500 KB, total page weight under 5 MB.

### 4.3 Pages and Collection Items
- **Add social sharing images and SEO descriptions** — every new page should have both.
- **Add tags and categories** — helps organise blog posts, products, and gallery items.
- **Link to internal site content** — connect related pages with internal links.
- **Customise URL slugs** — slugs should reflect page content.

---

## 5. Ranking Changes

- Monitor analytics for traffic trends after making SEO changes.
- Allow **one to two weeks** before expecting ranking improvements.
- If rankings decline, analyse keyword performance and consider targeting different search terms.
