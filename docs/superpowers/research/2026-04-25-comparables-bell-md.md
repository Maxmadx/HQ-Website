# Aircraft Comparison Research — Bell & MD Helicopter Families

**Date:** 2026-04-25
**Author:** Research pass for HQ Aviation "Aircraft Comparison" tool
**Currency conversion rate used throughout:** 1 USD = 0.7438 GBP (per EconomyPulse, 10 April 2026; April 2026 average ~0.7466). Historical Q2 2024 figures use ~0.79 GBP/USD as a sanity check where the source uses Q2 2024 list.
**Confidence framing:** HQ Aviation does NOT operate any of these aircraft, so every number below should be treated as **estimate** by the comparison tool. Operator-survey numbers are flagged where used; manufacturer marketing numbers are flagged separately. Where a number could not be sourced credibly, the field reads "needs further research" rather than an invented figure.

---

## 1) Bell 505 Jet Ranger X

In production. Single-engine turbine. Direct R66 rival in the light-turbine class.

**Acquisition price (new):** ~£1.39M GBP (~$1.86M USD)
- Source: AvBuyer / Aircraft Cost Calculator — cited "2026 advertised pricing $1.86mn" (https://www.aircraftcostcalculator.com/AircraftOperatingCosts/743/Bell+505+JetRanger+X)
- Note: $1.86M figure is widely repeated as base/list. Real-world delivered price with options, avionics packages, dealer fees, and UK delivery typically pushes higher. Some sources reference $3M+ for fully-optioned new builds. Convert at 0.7438: $1.86M × 0.7438 ≈ £1.38M base. Add 10–20% for typical UK-spec build / dealer.

**Used market price range (~5–15 yr example):** £820k – £1.85M GBP (US$1.1M – US$2.5M)
- Source: AvBuyer listings & Controller.com 2024–2025 asking prices, summarised: https://www.avbuyer.com/aircraft/helicopter/turbine/bell-helicopters/505-jetranger-x ; https://www.controller.com/listings/for-sale/bell/505-jet-ranger-x/aircraft
- Note: Pre-owned average ≈ $1.7M (Helivalues, https://www.helivalues.com/helicopter/values/information/prices/bell-505-jet-ranger-x). 5-yr-old example typically mid-pack; 10–15 yr range not yet meaningful (505 only certified 2017).

**Fuel burn (cruise):** ~30–32 US gph (Jet A-1) at 110–115 KTAS
- Source: Vertical Magazine flight review (https://verticalmag.com/features/back-in-the-game/) ; Pilot Magazine flight test (https://pilotweb.aero/aircraft/flight-test-bell-505-jet-ranger-x-6290766/)
- Note: Bell 505 product spec sheet contains the full fuel-flow vs airspeed chart (https://www.bellflight.com/-/media/site-specific/bell-flight/documents/products/505/bell-505-product-specifications.pdf). 30 gph is the operator-confirmed real-world figure.

**Fuel type:** Jet A-1
- Source: Bell 505 Product Specifications (Safran Arrius 2R turboshaft); Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bell_505_Jet_Ranger_X)

**Scheduled maintenance accrual (£/hr):** ~£155/hr ($209/hr airframe)
- Source: Bell-published owner-operator estimate cited in Philip Greenspun's Bell 505 review (https://philip.greenspun.com/flying/bell-505/review) — "Bell calculates $209 per hour for airframe maintenance"
- Note: Operator anecdotal — Aircraft Cost Calculator places "Total Variable Cost" around $465/hr (Conklin & de Decker basis): https://www.aircraftcostcalculator.com/AircraftOperatingCosts/743/Bell+505+JetRanger+X. Convert $209 × 0.7438 ≈ £155.

**Engine overhaul reserve (£/hr):** ~£70/hr ($94/hr per Bell)
- Source: Philip Greenspun review citing Bell figure (https://philip.greenspun.com/flying/bell-505/review)
- Note: Safran Arrius 2R TBO ≈ 3,000 hr (most-cited; one source quotes 4,000 hr): see Aerossurance / AOPA 505 guide (https://www.aopa.org/go-fly/aircraft-and-ownership/aircraft-guide/aircraft/bell-505). Engine overhaul reserve at $94/hr × 0.7438 ≈ £70.

**Airframe component reserves (£/hr):** needs further research
- Note: Bell does not publish a discrete "airframe component" reserve separate from airframe maintenance for the 505; the $209/hr "airframe" figure above likely already aggregates routine MX + life-limited part accrual. Conklin & de Decker is the canonical source for a clean component-only line item but is paywalled (https://conklindedecker.jetsupport.com/details/Bell%20505%20Jet%20Ranger%20X).

**UK private insurance (£/year, 100 hr/yr private):** ~£10,000 – £18,000 GBP
- Source: AJG UK / Horner Blakey general guidance (https://www.hornerblakey.co.uk/helicopter-insurance-everything-you-need-to-know/ ; https://www.ajg.com/uk/aerospace-aviation/helicopter-insurance/). BWI Robinson R44 quote of £5–20k as light-helicopter benchmark (https://bwifly.com/robinson-r44-insurance-cost/).
- Note: Confidence low — no Bell 505-specific UK quote was published. Estimate based on comparable single-turbine private hull £1.4M @ ~0.8% hull rate + liability premiums. **HQ should validate with their broker.**

**Annual inspection cost (£/yr):** ~£3,000 – £5,000 GBP
- Source: Operator anecdotal — extrapolated from BWI Bell 206 figures of $4–6k labour (https://bwifly.com/bell-206-operating-cost/). The 505 shares much of the 206 dynamic system family.
- Note: Confidence low — needs HQ shop input. Bell 505 maintenance planning info: https://www.bellcustomer.com/Bulletins/Download?FileName=505-Airworthiness_and_Scheduled_Inspection.pdf

**Hangarage UK typical (£/yr):** ~£6,000 – £15,000 GBP
- Source: Heli Air UK hangarage (https://www.heliair.com/helicopter-hangarage/) ; ICE Helicopters Elstree long-term (https://www.icehelicopters.com/long-term-overnight-helicopter-hangarage-london-elstree-aerodrome/)
- Note: Range reflects rural farmstrip (~£500/mo) up to a major aerodrome with serviced facilities (~£1,250/mo). 505 footprint ~12.1m × 2.6m so single-helicopter bay applies.

**Data confidence:** partial
**Open gaps:** Airframe component reserve breakdown; UK-specific private insurance quote; UK annual inspection cost from a Bell-approved CAA Part 145 shop.

## 2) Bell 206 JetRanger / Bell 206L LongRanger (USED MARKET ONLY)

Out of production (2010 last build). Massive global secondhand fleet. Treat all "new" lines as n/a.

**Acquisition price (new):** n/a — used market only
- Source: Bell ceased 206 production in 2010 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bell_206)

**Used market price range (~5–15 yr equivalent, but most fleet is 25–45 yr old):**
- **206B-3 JetRanger III:** £260k – £700k GBP (~$350k – $940k)
  - Source: Aircraft Bluebook Winter 2023 ($400k 1985 model → $900k 2009 model), as cited by AvBuyer summary (https://www.avbuyer.com/aircraft/helicopter/turbine/bell-helicopters/206b-iii). Trade-A-Plane current listings (https://www.trade-a-plane.com/search?make=BELL&model_group=BELL+206+SERIES&s-type=aircraft). UK-specific: 1985 206B III at £519,504 (Europlane Sales: https://europlanesales.com/aircraft-for-sale/bell-206b-jetranger-2/)
- **206L-3 LongRanger III:** £590k – £820k GBP (~$799k – $1.10M)
  - Source: Helivalues (https://www.helivalues.com/helicopter/values/information/prices/bell-206l-3-longranger-iii) ; Controller.com listings
- **206L-4 LongRanger IV:** £650k – £1.25M GBP (~$880k – $1.68M)
  - Source: Aircraftforsale.com 1994 206L-4 at $880k (https://aircraftforsale.com/l/bell-206l-4-longranger-iv/67b0da80-9b11-49ae-b0d4-34deb41a5499) ; Controller.com (https://www.controller.com/listings/for-sale/bell/206l-4/aircraft)
- Note: Hugely individualised by component time, life-limited parts status, and proximity to next overhaul. UK fleet typically commands a 5–10% premium over US comps once import VAT/CofV is factored.

**Fuel burn (cruise):**
- **206B-3:** ~25–28 US gph (Jet A-1) — operator-typical at 110 KIAS
  - Source: WorldWind Helicopters operator spec (http://www.wwheli.com/jetrangerspecifications.htm) ; AvBuyer 206B-3 vs EC120B comparison citing 25.2 gph (https://www.avbuyer.com/articles/helicopter-comparisons/bell-206b-3-jetranger-iii-vs-airbus-ec-120b-33898)
- **206L-3 / L-4:** ~30–38 US gph (Jet A-1)
  - Source: USDA Forest Service Flight Charter Awarded 2018-2021 spec sheet listing 38 gph for 206L-3/L-1 with C30P (https://www.fs.usda.gov/sites/default/files/media_wysiwyg/flt_chrt_awarded_2018-2021.pdf) — commercial-mission burn; private cruise typically 30 gph.

**Fuel type:** Jet A-1
- Source: Allison/Rolls-Royce 250-C20J (206B), 250-C30P (206L-3/L-4)

**Scheduled maintenance accrual (£/hr):** ~£12 – £25/hr ($15–$30/hr)
- Source: BWI operator survey (https://bwifly.com/bell-206-operating-cost/) — "$15–$30 per hour" routine maintenance reserve. MyAircraftCost (https://myaircraftcost.com/) corroborates.

**Engine overhaul reserve (£/hr):** ~£42/hr ($57/hr) for 206B; ~£60–80/hr for 206L (higher TBO + larger engine)
- Source: BWI Bell 206 cost guide (https://bwifly.com/bell-206-operating-cost/) — "overhaul cost typically $200,000 → $57/hr"
- Note: 250-C20J TBO 3,000–3,600 hr (per Rolls-Royce M250 commercial service data: https://customers.rolls-royce.com/cs/idcplg?IdcService=PSA_GET_FILE&dDocName=RRC026286). 250-C30P TBO 3,500 hr; overhaul $250–300k typical.

**Airframe component reserves (£/hr):** ~£40 – £80/hr
- Source: Bolt Flight 206B II cost summary (https://boltflight.com/bell-206b-ii-price-and-operating-costs/) ; Aircraft Cost Calculator 206B3 (https://www.aircraftcostcalculator.com/AircraftOperatingCosts/426/Bell+206B3)
- Note: Anecdotal range — main rotor blade life limit, mast, transmission overhaul (~5,000 hr) and tail rotor drivetrain dominate. Operator surveys vary widely by airframe age.

**UK private insurance (£/year, 100 hr/yr private):** ~£4,000 – £12,000 GBP (BWI cites $5–15k for 206 series)
- Source: BWI Bell 206 (https://bwifly.com/bell-206-operating-cost/) — "$5,000 to $15,000 depending on coverage"
- Note: Older airframe = lower hull value = lower premium absolute, often higher rate %. Confidence low for UK specifically; **HQ should validate with their broker.**

**Annual inspection cost (£/yr):** ~£3,000 – £4,500 GBP ($4,000–$6,000 labour)
- Source: BWI Bell 206 (https://bwifly.com/bell-206-operating-cost/) — "$4,000–$6,000 in labor"
- Note: Parts on top can easily double this for older airframes.

**Hangarage UK typical (£/yr):** ~£6,000 – £15,000 GBP
- Source: Heli Air UK (https://www.heliair.com/helicopter-hangarage/) ; PistonHeads UK PPL discussion (https://www.pistonheads.com/gassing/topic.asp?h=0&f=191&t=1336600) — £200–£500/mo typical
- Note: 206L slightly larger footprint than B-3; still single-bay light-helicopter pricing.

**Data confidence:** partial — pricing well-sourced, MX reserves anecdotal
**Open gaps:** UK-specific insurance quote bands; airframe component reserve clean breakdown for 206L-4.

## 3) Bell 407 (in production)

Single-engine turbine, medium-light. R88 territory.

**Acquisition price (new):** ~£2.45M – £2.60M GBP (~$3.3M – $3.5M USD for 407GXi)
- Source: 2025 listings cited via FairLifts pricing guide and Aircraft Cost Calculator (https://www.aircraftcostcalculator.com/AircraftOperatingCosts/679/Bell+407+GXP); Helivalues (https://www.helivalues.com/helicopter/values/information/prices/bell-407gxi)
- Note: Some sources say $3.0M average new (https://www.aircraftcostcalculator.com/AircraftOperatingCosts/678/Bell+407GX); newer GXi build typically $3.3–3.5M. Convert at 0.7438.

**Used market price range (~5–15 yr example):** £1.5M – £2.5M GBP (~$2.0M – $3.4M)
- Source: AvBuyer 407 listings (https://www.avbuyer.com/aircraft/helicopter/turbine/bell-helicopters/407) ; Controller.com (https://www.controller.com/listings/for-sale/bell/407/aircraft) ; Helivalues — 30-yr average $2.9M.
- Note: 407GX/GXi premium over base 407.

**Fuel burn (cruise):** ~43 US gph (Jet A-1) at 130 KTAS
- Source: GlobalAir specs (https://www.globalair.com/aircraft-for-sale/specifications?specid=89) ; AOPA Bell 407 (https://www.aopa.org/go-fly/aircraft-and-ownership/aircraft-guide/aircraft/bell-407)
- Note: Bell 407GXi product spec sheet has fuel-flow vs airspeed charts (https://www.bellflight.com/-/media/site-specific/bell-flight/documents/products/407-gxi/bell-407gxi-product-specifications.pdf). 43.2 gph commonly cited.

**Fuel type:** Jet A-1 (Rolls-Royce 250-C47B/8 or C47E/4)
- Source: Bellflight (https://www.bellflight.com/products/bell-407)

**Scheduled maintenance accrual (£/hr):** ~£187/hr ($252/hr airframe)
- Source: Bolt Flight 407 GXi DOC breakdown (https://boltflight.com/bell-407-gxi-price-and-operating-costs/) — "Airframe Maintenance: ~$252/hr"
- Note: Confidence partial — operator survey average; Conklin & de Decker has the canonical figure but is paywalled.

**Engine overhaul reserve (£/hr):** ~£95/hr ($128/hr powerplant)
- Source: Bolt Flight (https://boltflight.com/bell-407-gxi-price-and-operating-costs/) — "Powerplant Maintenance: ~$128/hr"
- Note: 250-C47E TBO and overhaul costs not publicly itemised; transmission overhaul extended to 5,000 hr per Bell (https://www.rolls-royce.com/media/press-releases/2018/26-02-2018-rr-m250-c47e-engine-to-power-new-bell-407gxi-helicopter.aspx).

**Airframe component reserves (£/hr):** needs further research
- Note: 407 main transmission 5,000 hr TBO is published; component reserves not separated in public sources. Conklin & de Decker (https://conklindedecker.jetsupport.com/details/Bell%20407GXi) paywalled.

**UK private insurance (£/year, 100 hr/yr private):** ~£18,000 – £30,000 GBP
- Source: Estimated from light-medium turbine bracket per AJG / Horner Blakey general guidance (https://www.ajg.com/uk/aerospace-aviation/helicopter-insurance/). No 407-specific UK quote published.
- Note: Hull value £2M+ pushes premium materially over 505. Confidence low — **HQ should validate with their broker.**

**Annual inspection cost (£/yr):** ~£5,000 – £8,000 GBP
- Source: Anecdotal extrapolation from 206-series figures + 407 complexity uplift. No explicit public source.

**Hangarage UK typical (£/yr):** ~£8,000 – £18,000 GBP
- Source: Heli Air UK / ICE Helicopters Elstree (as above)
- Note: 407 length 12.7m × rotor 10.7m — single light-helicopter bay still applies.

**Data confidence:** partial
**Open gaps:** Airframe component reserve breakdown; UK insurance + UK annual inspection from a Bell-approved Part 145 shop.

## 4) Bell 429 (in production, twin-engine)

Twin turbine, light-medium. Premium executive / EMS / police.

**Acquisition price (new):** ~£4.76M GBP (~$6.4M USD)
- Source: Aircraft Cost Calculator (https://www.aircraftcostcalculator.com/AircraftOperatingCosts/396/Bell+429) — "$6,400,000 USD"
- Note: Designer Series with full executive interior commonly $10M+ (https://the-jet-collection.com/aicraft/bell-429/for-sale/). Convert $6.4M × 0.7438 ≈ £4.76M base.

**Used market price range (~5–15 yr example):** £2.8M – £4.0M GBP (~$3.8M – $5.4M)
- Source: AvBuyer 429 listings (https://www.avbuyer.com/aircraft/helicopter/turbine/bell-helicopters/429) ; The Jet Collection 429 used (https://the-jet-collection.com/aicraft/bell-429/for-sale/) — pre-owned avg $4.95M.
- Note: 429 first delivered 2009 so 5–15 yr range is meaningful.

**Fuel burn (cruise):** ~70–75 US gph total (both engines combined, Jet A-1) at 145–150 KTAS
- Source: Aviation Week aircraft overview (https://aviationweek.com/business-aviation/aircraft-overview/aircraft-overview-bell-429) ; Vertical Reference Forums fuel-burn thread (https://helicopterforum.verticalreference.com/topic/22018-bell-429-fuel-burn/) — operator anecdotal
- Note: Bell 429 product spec sheet contains the full fuel-flow chart (https://www.bellflight.com/-/media/site-specific/bell-flight/documents/products/429/bell-429-product-specifications.pdf). Operator anecdote in forum: ~"1,000 lb/hr" at high cruise → roughly 150 lb / 5.5 lb-per-gal Jet A ≈ 27 gph per engine = ~55 gph low cruise. Best-published combined cruise burn ~70 gph at high cruise; 1,000 lb/hr ≈ 150 gph appears to be max-power, not cruise.

**Fuel type:** Jet A-1 (twin Pratt & Whitney Canada PW207D1)
- Source: Bellflight (https://www.bellflight.com/products/bell-429)

**Scheduled maintenance accrual (£/hr):** ~£190 – £250/hr
- Source: Everette Aviation (https://everetteaviation.com/cost-and-value-of-your-bell-429/) — annual MX $75–100k for typical utilisation; The Jet Collection (https://the-jet-collection.com/aicraft/bell-429/operating-costs/)
- Note: Confidence partial. Conklin & de Decker (https://conklindedecker.jetsupport.com/details/Bell%20429) paywalled.

**Engine overhaul reserve (£/hr):** ~£110 – £150/hr (combined for both PW207D engines)
- Source: PW207D TBO 4,000 hr per BoltFlight 429 review (https://boltflight.com/bell-429-globalranger-a-comprehensive-review-of-a-versatile-helicopter/) ; Aircraft Cost Calculator 429 (https://www.aircraftcostcalculator.com/AircraftOperatingCosts/396/Bell+429)
- Note: Twin-engine overhauls cost roughly 2× single. Reserves expected to land in the $150–200/hr range total.

**Airframe component reserves (£/hr):** ~£90 – £150/hr (estimate)
- Source: Estimated from total maintenance reserves of $333–444/hr per AvBuyer / Aircraft Cost Calculator after subtracting engine + scheduled airframe components.
- Note: Main transmission 5,000 hr TBO; tail rotor gearbox 3,200 hr (https://boltflight.com/bell-429-globalranger-a-comprehensive-review-of-a-versatile-helicopter/). Confidence low — needs Conklin & de Decker access.

**UK private insurance (£/year, 100 hr/yr private):** ~£40,000 – £75,000 GBP
- Source: Estimate from twin-turbine premium bracket per AJG (https://www.ajg.com/uk/aerospace-aviation/helicopter-insurance/). BWI guidance "tens of thousands for twin-engine".
- Note: Hull £4M+ at ~1.0–1.5% rate plus liability. **Strongly recommend HQ broker validation.**

**Annual inspection cost (£/yr):** ~£12,000 – £20,000 GBP
- Source: Anecdotal extrapolation; no public Bell 429 UK figure surfaced.

**Hangarage UK typical (£/yr):** ~£12,000 – £25,000 GBP
- Source: Heli Air UK (https://www.heliair.com/helicopter-hangarage/)
- Note: 429 footprint 13.1m × 2.6m needs medium-helicopter bay; pricing scales with rotor diameter and overall footprint per Heli Air pricing approach.

**Data confidence:** partial
**Open gaps:** Clean PW207D-specific reserve numbers; UK private insurance quote; UK annual inspection cost.

## 5) MD500E / MD520N (MD500 series)

MD500E technically still in low-rate production at MD Helicopters. MD520N (NOTAR) ended production around 2012. Most UK market is used.

**Acquisition price (new):** ~£1.41M GBP (~$1.9M USD list, MD500E only — Q2 2024 list price)
- Source: Aircraft Cost Calculator MD500E (https://www.aircraftcostcalculator.com/AircraftOperatingCosts/522/MD+500E) — "Q2 2024 list price $1,900,000"; MD Helicopters MD500E product page (https://www.mdhelicopters.com/models/md-500e/)
- Note: MD520N: n/a — out of production. Convert $1.9M × 0.7438 ≈ £1.41M. Real-world MD has had production volatility post-2022; new-build delivery slots scarce.

**Used market price range (~5–15 yr equivalent — most fleet 25–40 yr):**
- **MD500E:** £635k – £1.85M GBP (~$850k – $2.5M)
  - Source: HeliTrader MD500E listings (https://helitrader.com/for-sale/helicopters/md-helicopters/md500e/) ; AvBuyer 500E (https://www.avbuyer.com/aircraft/helicopter/turbine/mcdonnell-douglas-helicopter/500e) ; UK Aviation Sales (https://www.ukaviationsales.com/aircraft-for-sale/md500e/)
  - UK-specific: 1988 MD500E at $950k via UK showroom (Planecheck https://www.planecheck.com/aspdet.asp?nr=62962)
- **MD520N:** £450k – £900k GBP (~$600k – $1.2M); thinner market
  - Source: HeliTrader MD520N listed at $780k (https://helitrader.com/for-sale/helicopters/md-helicopters/md500/); limited liquidity.

**Fuel burn (cruise):** ~22–28 US gph (Jet A-1) at 110–125 KTAS
- Source: MD Helicopters MD500 series technical description (https://www.manualslib.com/manual/839482/Md-Helicopters-Md500-Series.html?page=17) ; Vertical Reference forum operator data (https://helicopterforum.verticalreference.com/topic/26720-fuel-consumption-on-a-md500e-rolls-royce-250-c20r/) — "30.5 gph at 110 kt" (high-power); operator-typical normal cruise 22–28 gph.
- Note: MD 500E product spec PDF (https://www.mdhelicopters.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/MD_500E_Product_Spec.pdf). MD520N similar.

**Fuel type:** Jet A-1 (Allison/Rolls-Royce 250-C20B on MD500E and MD520N)
- Source: Wikipedia MD Helicopters MD500 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MD_Helicopters_MD_500)

**Scheduled maintenance accrual (£/hr):** ~£75/hr ($101/hr airframe per Conklin & de Decker)
- Source: Conklin & de Decker MD500E summary cited in operator forum (https://canadianhelicopterforum.ca/topic/1927-cost-of-operation/) — "total airframe cost $101.44/hr"; airframe TBO reserves $26.48/hr + LLP reserves $25.29/hr.
- Note: Convert $101 × 0.7438 ≈ £75.

**Engine overhaul reserve (£/hr):** ~£33/hr ($45/hr)
- Source: Conklin & de Decker MD500E figure per forum citation (https://canadianhelicopterforum.ca/topic/1927-cost-of-operation/) — "engine overhaul, spares and accessories: $44.86/hr"
- Note: 250-C20B TBO ~3,500 hr (Rolls-Royce M250 Series 2 turbine and compressor, https://helicoptermaintenancemagazine.com/article/rolls-royce-m250-engine-lessons-field).

**Airframe component reserves (£/hr):** ~£20/hr ($26.48/hr TBO components) + £19/hr ($25.29/hr LLP) = ~£39/hr
- Source: Conklin & de Decker via operator forum citation (https://canadianhelicopterforum.ca/topic/1927-cost-of-operation/)
- Note: Likely the cleanest published component-reserve breakdown of any aircraft on this list.

**UK private insurance (£/year, 100 hr/yr private):** ~£8,000 – £15,000 GBP
- Source: Estimated from Bell 206 comparable bracket per BWI (https://bwifly.com/bell-206-operating-cost/) — MD500E hull values broadly similar to 206B-3.
- Note: Confidence low; MD500E has higher accident rate historically which can push premium. **HQ broker validation needed.**

**Annual inspection cost (£/yr):** ~£3,500 – £5,500 GBP
- Source: Anecdotal extrapolation from 206-series; MD500E sometimes cited as cheaper to inspect due to simpler dynamic system.

**Hangarage UK typical (£/yr):** ~£5,000 – £12,000 GBP
- Source: Heli Air UK (https://www.heliair.com/helicopter-hangarage/)
- Note: MD500E compact (footprint 9.4m × 2.0m) so often fits smaller bays cheaper than 206/505/407.

**Data confidence:** partial — MX reserves better-sourced than insurance/hangarage
**Open gaps:** MD520N-specific reserves (likely similar to 500E but NOTAR drive shaft inspections add cost); UK insurance quote.

## Summary Notes for the Comparison Tool

1. **Single biggest data gap across all five aircraft is UK private-operator insurance.** Public sources only give wide ranges; a 5-minute call from HQ to Hayward Aviation, AJG, or Horner Blakey would yield real quote bands and replace these estimates.
2. **The Bell 206 series is by far the deepest used market** with the widest reserve-cost variability — mostly because individual aircraft component-time status drives more value than year of build. Recommend the comparison tool surface this caveat.
3. **MD500E has the cleanest publicly-reported component reserves** thanks to one Conklin & de Decker excerpt that surfaced via the Canadian Helicopter Forum (https://canadianhelicopterforum.ca/topic/1927-cost-of-operation/). Worth quoting verbatim.
4. **Bell 505 figures should be most directly comparable to R66** in the existing HQ tool, since both are single-Safran-class light turbines targeting the same buyer.
5. **Bell 429 is in a different cost league** and should arguably be flagged in the UI as a step-change product (twin, much higher hull, higher insurance, twice the engine reserve) so private buyers don't get a misleading apples-to-apples comparison.
6. **Conklin & de Decker is the canonical source for clean reserve breakdowns but is paywalled.** HQ may want to invest in a single-aircraft Conklin report for each comparable to strengthen the numbers — typical cost is a few hundred USD per report and it would convert every "estimate" tag here to "verified".
